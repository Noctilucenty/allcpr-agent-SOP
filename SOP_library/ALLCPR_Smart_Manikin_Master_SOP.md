# ALLCPR Smart Manikin — Master SOP

**A simple, step-by-step guide for staff and users**

Version 1.0 · English only

---

## Short intro

This is the combined, plain-English manual for the ALLCPR Smart Manikin Learning Station.

It brings together the useful parts of the ALLCPR SOP library into one place so you can:

- **Inspect** a site quickly and consistently
- **Prepare** a station for class
- **Document** what you find (with photos)
- **Escalate** problems safely — without repairing or dismantling equipment

Read the section you need. Follow the checklist. Take photos. When in doubt, **report it — do not try to fix hardware yourself.**

> **Golden rule:** Your job is to **check, clean (lightly), document, and report** — **not** to repair, open, or rewire equipment. If something is broken or unsafe, photograph it and escalate to ALLCPR.

## 3. Core Safety Rules

These rules apply to **every** section of this manual.

> ### ✅ DO
> - Take **before** photos and **after** photos.
> - Do only **safe, simple** cleanup and fixes.
> - Write down every problem, even if you fixed it.
> - Report unresolved issues to ALLCPR.
> - Stop and put people first if there is any danger.

> ### 🚫 DO NOT
> - Do **not** repair, dismantle, open, rewire, or replace equipment unless this SOP explicitly allows it.
> - Do **not** force doors, locks, lockboxes, or keypads.
> - Do **not** share passcodes, lockbox codes, Wi-Fi passwords, or staff PINs.
> - Do **not** use old, staged, or replacement photos.
> - Do **not** guess codes or invent repair steps.

> ### 🔺 WHEN TO ESCALATE
> Escalate to ALLCPR / a supervisor for any **safety, access, lease, refund, insurance, cost, or legal** issue.
> Staff never self-approve these — always hand them to the official approval path.

## 4. Staff Full Site Inspection Workflow

**Who:** Staff / site representative (staff PIN required).
**Not for:** Students (students use [Section 5](#5-student-quick-class-readiness-check)).
**Record form:** Weekly Site Check Report.
**How often:** At least once per week. Inspect more often if ALLCPR sends notice, a student complains, or something looks abnormal.

Follow these 12 steps **in order**.

---

### Step 1 — Arrive and pause before touching setup

- **Check:** Look at the whole room and the Smart Manikin station first. Do not clean, move, fix, or organize anything yet.
- **Evidence:** None yet — get ready to photograph the site in its found state.
- ✅ **Allowed:** Observing, planning your photos.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Cleaning, moving, or starting repairs before before-photos.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** The room is unsafe (water/electric hazard, fire, injury) — stop, follow [Section 3](#3-core-safety-rules) / safety handling, then report to ALLCPR.

### Step 2 — Take before photos first

- **Check:** Photograph the site **before** any cleaning or organizing.
- **Evidence — take a "before" photo of each:**
  - Whole room
  - Smart Manikin area
  - Supplies / consumables area
  - Door or signage
  - Any abnormal / problem area
- ✅ **Allowed:** Real photos of the current state.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Old, staged, or replacement photos. Cleaning or moving anything before these photos.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** You cannot safely photograph an area — note why and continue; never enter an unsafe area for a photo.

### Step 3 — Table / station pre-check

- **Check:** Before moving anything, compare the station to the **standard placement reference** (see [Section 6](#6-table--station-setup) diagram). Confirm each item is present and in its standard spot:
  - iPad · Smart Manikin(s) · AED Training Pads · consumables area
  - Tissues / simulated gauze (if supplied) · disinfecting wipes · disposable gloves · mask adaptors · face shield (optional) · Pocket Mask · BVM
  - iPad connected to power · Smart Manikin connected to power
  - Items in standard placement, not scattered
- **Evidence:** Mark each item **Present / Missing / Problem**. Photograph any missing or problem item (if safe).
- ✅ **Allowed:** Comparing against the diagram; noting what is missing or wrong.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Inventing a replacement or repair; dismantling the manikin/iPad; treating the diagram as repair instructions.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** An item is missing/broken and cannot be fixed on site — mark it, photograph it, note it, and report to ALLCPR.

### Step 4 — Cleanliness check

- **Check:** Overall hygiene. Empty the trash and replace the bag if needed. Keep the floor clean and clear visible mess.
- **Evidence:** Record cleanliness status (OK / fixed / problem). Do not rely only on the photo.
- ✅ **Allowed:** Light cleaning, trash removal, tidying.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Skipping the written record just because you cleaned it.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** Cleaning cannot be completed on site — note it and include it in the report.

### Step 5 — Supplies check

- **Check:** Confirm disinfecting supplies are **enough for one week**. Disinfect all teaching equipment. Note any low or missing consumables (wipes, gloves, mask adaptors).
- **Evidence:** Record supplies status; note low/missing consumables.
- ✅ **Allowed:** Disinfecting equipment; recording restock needs.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Making a student responsible for staff disinfection or restocking.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** You cannot restock on site — mark it and include the restock need in the report.

### Step 6 — Equipment check

- **Check:** Smart Manikin, iPad, AED Pads, BVM, and Pocket Mask are complete and working. iPad and Smart Manikin are connected to power. Equipment is in the standard position.
- **Evidence:** Record equipment status. Photograph any broken or unresponsive device.
- ✅ **Allowed:** Confirming power cables are connected; noting symptoms.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Dismantling or repairing the manikin, iPad, camera, or access control; improvising an undocumented reset or calibration.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** A device is broken or unresponsive — photograph it, note the symptom, and report to ALLCPR.

### Step 7 — Access / camera / Wi-Fi / signage / safety checks

- **Check:** Door access is normal; camera is online; Wi-Fi is normal; signs are complete and clear; no visible water/electric/equipment hazards.
- **Evidence:** Record access / camera / Wi-Fi / signage / safety status.
- ✅ **Allowed:** Confirming things work; staff unlock **internal** access only when needed.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Forcing a door, guessing passcodes, or sharing access codes outside internal operations.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** There is a safety concern (stop and avoid unsafe use), or access fails and class is delayed — record attempts and report to ALLCPR.

### Step 8 — Safe-only fixes

- **Check:** If an issue can be **safely** fixed on site, fix it and document what you did.
- **Evidence:** Record the action taken. Take before/after photos of the fix if any.
- ✅ **Allowed:** Simple, safe fixes (tidying, trash, restocking a supply you have).
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Dismantling or repairing the manikin, iPad, camera, or access control.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** It cannot be fixed on site — photograph it, note it, and report to ALLCPR promptly.

### Step 9 — Take after photos

- **Check:** After all inspection and cleaning is complete, photograph the site again. Keep angles as close as possible to the before photos.
- **Evidence — take an "after" photo of each:**
  - Whole room · Smart Manikin area · supplies area · door or signage
  - If you fixed an issue, photograph the corrected result.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Old or staged photos.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** An area still has an unresolved issue — keep the problem photo for the report.

### Step 10 — Complete the Weekly Site Check Report

- **Check:** Fill in the report (see [Section 15](#15-weekly-site-check-report)): site result, problems found, actions taken, whether ALLCPR support is needed.
- **Evidence:** Completed Weekly Site Check Report. Everything must be truthful and complete.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Leaving the report incomplete or entering false information.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** Any unresolved problem remains — record it and mark that ALLCPR support is needed.

### Step 11 — Upload / report completion

- **Check:** Upload before photos, after photos, the completed report, and any issue photos to the correct place. Recommended: same day as the inspection.
- **Evidence:** Before photos, after photos, report, and issue photos uploaded.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Uploading old or staged photos; skipping the upload.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** Upload keeps failing — retry and note the delay.

> ℹ️ The upload destination is a **per-site Google Drive folder**. This is **not managed by the app** — the app records "upload completed" but does not manage Google Drive itself. *(Needs staff confirmation of the current folder for your site.)*

### Step 12 — Final log

- **Check:** Set the final status:
  - `completed` — no unresolved issue remains
  - `needs_support` — an unresolved issue remains
  - `safety_escalation` — there is a safety issue
  - Include the missing/problem counts.
- 🚫 **Not allowed:** Marking `completed` while an unresolved or safety issue remains.
- 🔺 **Escalate when:** Any unresolved or safety issue exists — it must go to ALLCPR, not close silently.

## 5. Student Quick Class Readiness Check

**Who:** Students / anyone starting a class.
**Type:** Report-only. **No PIN needed.**

This is a quick "is the class ready?" look. Students **report** problems — they do **not** repair or move equipment beyond simple, safe checks. **If setup looks wrong, report it to staff.**

**Quick 7-point check — just look and report:**

1. **Can you enter?** Report if you cannot get in (add site + class time).
2. **Does the room look safe and usable?** Report anything unsafe, messy, or blocked.
3. **Can you see the Smart Manikin / iPad station?** Report if it is missing or not set up.
4. **Does the iPad / app open?** Report a black screen, no response, or "won't open."
5. **Does the Smart Manikin look connected / usable?** Report a Bluetooth / connection / data problem.
6. **Anything missing, dirty, or broken?** Describe it. Photo/video only if safe.
7. **Submit the quick report** for staff to review.

> ### ✅ DO (students)
> - Look, check, and report.
> - Take a photo only if it is safe.
> - Submit a **safety escalation** and stop using the area if anything is unsafe.

> ### 🚫 DO NOT (students)
> - Do not force entry or use staff-only access.
> - Do not move, reset, repair, or dismantle equipment.
> - Do not handle staff-only duties (Weekly Report, Google Drive upload, passcodes, lockbox codes, cleaning/supply duties).

*(See the equipment placement image in [Section 6](#6-table--station-setup) if you want to know what a correct station looks like.)*

## 7. Tablet / iPad Power or Charging Issues

Use this for: *"iPad no battery," "tablet dead," "won't charge," "won't power on."*

> ⚠️ The source SOP records **no** iPad/tablet battery or power repair method. Do **not** open or repair the device. Confirm it is charging, save evidence, and escalate.

**Steps:**

1. **Check the visible charging / power connection** — is the cable plugged into the device and the outlet/power strip?
2. **Confirm the tablet / iPad is connected to power** (the SOP requires the iPad to stay connected to power).
3. **Wait briefly** for a charging or power indicator.
4. **If still not usable, take photo evidence** — the screen, the charging cable, and the power/charging state.
5. **Mark / report the station or device as not ready.**
6. **Include the issue** in the inspection / Weekly Site Check Report.
7. **Escalate to ALLCPR support / staff** (and engineer/vendor); notify a supervisor if class is affected.

> ### 🚫 DO NOT
> - Do not open the device.
> - Do not repair or dismantle it.
> - Do not replace parts.
> - Do not handle unsafe electrical parts.
> - Do not claim the source has an iPad/tablet power fix, or invent a reset/calibration step.

## 8. Launch App / Black Screen / Page Not Loading

Use this for: *"Launch App opens a black tab," "app won't load," "screen is black," "app self-restarted and lost progress."*

**Steps:**

1. **If Launch App opens a black tab or the session does not load, reload / refresh once and wait.**
2. **If the reload fixes it,** continue and note it.
3. **If the reload does not fix it,** the source records this issue but **no documented fix** — collect evidence and escalate.
   - Save a photo/video of the black screen or restart, the exact time, the course step, and power/display/Bluetooth/tablet-PAD status.
   - Escalate to engineer/vendor; notify a supervisor if class is affected.

> ### 🚫 DO NOT
> - Do not invent browser or device repair steps.
> - Do not try undocumented reset, recovery, or calibration steps.

---

## 9. Bluetooth / Manikin Connection Issues

Use this for: *"Bluetooth won't connect," "manikin cannot connect," "PAD connected but training shows no data."*

**Steps:**

1. **Identify the connection issue** — will not connect, or connected but no training data.
2. **Follow the SOP-safe power steps (documented):**
   - Keep the manikin **and** the PAD powered with a power strip.
   - If it still will not connect while plugged in, use **only** the source-recorded manikin restart step.
   - Note: when powered-but-unplugged, the PAD may show *connected* but TRAINING receives no data — keeping it powered with the power strip is the documented step.
3. **If it is still unresolved,** save photo/video of the connected/no-data state and the exact symptom + time, then **document and escalate** to engineer/vendor; notify a supervisor if class is affected.

> ### 🚫 DO NOT
> - Do not dismantle equipment.
> - Do not invent unsupported reset, calibration, browser, or permission fixes.

## 10. Camera / Browser Permission Issues

Use this for: *"camera permission denied," "browser is blocking the camera."*

> ⚠️ The source SOP does **not** provide a documented browser/camera permission repair for the device session. Do not treat Wi-Fi/browser permissions as the documented device-session fix.

**Steps:**

1. **Check the browser / camera permission** only if the SOP supports it for your situation (a basic in-app permission prompt).
2. **Do not perform unsupported technical fixes** — no reinstalling, re-flashing, or device changes.
3. **If it is still unresolved,** capture the exact error text / screenshot, then **document and escalate** to engineer/vendor / tech support; notify a supervisor if class is affected.

> ### 🚫 DO NOT
> - Do not invent browser-permission, Wi-Fi, or calibration fixes.
> - Do not change device or system settings beyond a simple in-app permission prompt.

## 11. Wi-Fi / Internet / Facility Power Issues

Tell these two apart — they are **not** the same as a tablet battery problem (see [Section 7](#7-tablet--ipad-power-or-charging-issues) for battery).

### A. Wi-Fi / internet issue

1. **Confirm scope** — is it one device, the whole room/building network, or the course/platform?
2. **Basic authorized check** — if authorized, confirm the site network device has power; otherwise contact venue/property or tech support.
3. **Capture evidence** — screenshots of Wi-Fi/network errors, loading failures, and the exact error text.
4. **Escalate** — venue/property for site-network issues; supervisor/tech for platform/system issues.

### B. Facility power / outlet issue

1. **Safety first** — confirm no fire, smoke, burning smell, shock risk, injury, or unsafe dark-room movement before collecting photos.
2. **Confirm scope** — one room, the whole building, or the surrounding area?
3. **Ask venue/property** to inspect or reset authorized electrical equipment and give an estimated restore time.
4. **Prepare the escalation packet** — outage start time, class/site, affected headcount, evidence, venue response, and whether class can continue.

> ### 🚫 DO NOT
> - Do not handle unsafe electrical parts.
> - Do not operate breakers or rewire / repair outlets or electrical equipment yourself.
> - Do not change unauthorized network equipment.
> - Do not promise restore time, refund, reschedule, or compensation before approval.

> ### 🔺 WHEN TO ESCALATE
> If Wi-Fi, internet, or facility power is unresolved and class is affected — document and escalate to venue/property and a supervisor.

## 12. Door / Facility Access Issues

Use this for: *"doors not working," "locked out," "keypad won't open," "can't get in."*

**Steps:**

1. **Do not force** doors, locks, keypads, or lockboxes.
2. **Confirm the location / room** if it is safe (right address, building, floor, and room).
3. **Document the access issue** — record arrival time and every attempt.
4. **Report to ALLCPR staff / supervisor.**
5. **Mark the site / session "not ready"** if access is blocked.
6. **Never reveal or guess codes.**

> ### 🚫 DO NOT
> - Do not force the door.
> - Do not guess passcodes (including from images).
> - Do not share access codes outside internal operations.

> ### 🔺 WHEN TO ESCALATE
> If access fails, contact venue/property to open or confirm the entry method, then escalate to a supervisor if class is delayed.

## 16. New Site Assessment Reference

> 📄 **Reference only.** This is the BD new-location development process (SOP SM-BD-SOP-001, v1.1). It is **not** part of the normal staff site inspection and needs staff/product sign-off before it becomes an active flow. (Who: BD executor / ICPIS / partner, BD lead, management.)

**Core principle:** "region first, then site; information first, then judgment." For a low-staff / partly unattended model, **safety, easy findability, access-control and camera install, stable Wi-Fi, and parking usually matter more than rent alone.** This process does **not** replace the Field Assessment Form — that form stays the record of truth for on-site fields.

**Roles:**

- **BD executor / ICPIS / partner** — propose ZipCodes, find L1 sites, visit, fill the field form, upload photos/video, update the analysis sheet.
- **BD lead** — review lists, set visit priority, check evidence completeness, decide whether to submit to management.
- **Management** — final decision using the analysis sheet, evidence, lease terms, safety, and operational fit.
- **Support teams** — Operations, LMS/IT, Marketing/SEO, Admin/Finance join once a site is approved.

**The 12 stages, in order:**

| Stage | Owner | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. ZipCode screening | BD executor | Candidate ZipCode List (mark each A / B / C) |
| 2. Potential ZipCode confirmation | BD lead | Potential ZipCode List |
| 3. L1 site collection | BD executor | Scoring sheet — basic-info section |
| 4. Visit list confirmation | BD lead | Visit First / Call First / Need More Info / Hold / Reject |
| 5. Field assessment | BD executor | Completed field form (scanned) + photos + video to site-funnel folder |
| 6. Analysis sheet update (within 24h) | BD executor | Scoring sheet completed; media uploaded |
| 7. BD review | BD lead | Submit to Management / Need More Info / Hold / Reject |
| 8. Virtual course & site test | BD lead / Marketing | Ad + signup test result (see threshold) |
| 9. Management approval | Management | Approved to Proceed / Conditional / Hold / Rejected |
| 10. Equipment purchase & renovation prep | Finance / BD lead | Purchase list, payment, install plan |
| 11. Opening date & official ad launch | BD lead / Marketing | Opening date; ads / SEO / listing live |
| 12. Pre-open acceptance, system test, opening | LMS-IT / BD lead | Acceptance + system-test records, official opening |

**Site scoring decision bands:** ≥85 Priority Candidate · 75–84.9 Management Review · 65–74.9 Hold / Compare · <65 Reject.

**Open-the-site test threshold (stage 8):** run CPS ads for 3 days (test **weekday and weekend**) and Google Ads for 7 days. If a single week gets **more than 5 signups**, or **total signups reach 10**, the site may open. Below that, management decides.

> ### 🚫 Hard-elimination conditions (do not proceed; do not raise the score to pass)
> - Access control cannot be installed, or students cannot enter in a controlled way.
> - A camera cannot be installed, or it cannot cover the entrance and equipment area.
> - Wi-Fi / network cannot meet equipment needs and cannot be fixed by installing network.
> - Clear surrounding safety risk for evening or weekend student entry.
> - Landlord / property uncooperative, or major limits on access, camera, signage, or equipment placement.
> - Educational / training use not allowed, or lease terms would block normal operation.
> - Room size, flow, or environment clearly unsuitable for students to train independently.

> ### 🔺 WHEN TO ESCALATE / DO NOT self-approve
> Before management confirms the test passed: do **not** sign a lease, pay a deposit, promise a long term, accept extra fees or insurance, promise renovation, or buy equipment.
> Anything touching cost, liability, contract, insurance, safety, or operations goes to the BD lead and management. **Escalate to ALLCPR support** for the current official version.

## 17. Business Trip Reference

> 📄 **Reference only.** Relevant **only** when staff travel. Kept **separate** from the site-inspection flow. Needs staff/product sign-off. (Who: staff, staff PIN.)

The short-term business trip process, in order:

1. **Trip request** — submit the Business Trip Request form (do not book before approval).
2. **Approval** — record the approval decision (no self-approval).
3. **Pre-trip checklist** — complete the itinerary and pre-trip checklist.
4. **Daily activity log** — log daily activity and keep evidence.
5. **Trip report** — complete the trip report.
6. **Expense package** — assemble the expense package and apply the mileage reimbursement policy (do not claim outside policy).
7. **Follow-up list** — complete the follow-up action list.

### Mileage reimbursement policy (for ALLCPR instructors)

- **Eligibility:** applies when you travel **more than 20 miles one way** for work. Reimbursement covers only the miles **beyond the first 20 miles each way**.
- **Rate:** **$0.20 per eligible mile.**
- **Worked example:** a 70-mile one-way trip → 70 − 20 = 50 eligible miles one way → round trip 50 × 2 = 100 miles → 100 × $0.20 = **$20.00**.
- **Process:** submit via **Ramp** using the standard expense procedure. Include travel date(s), trip purpose, start and end locations, total miles, and the reimbursable-miles calculation. Provide supporting documents if requested.
- **Deadline:** submit **within 30 days** of travel. Late claims may be reviewed or denied.
- **Notes:** personal vehicles only. The policy is reviewed periodically based on fuel costs and operational needs.

## 18. Escalation Matrix

| Issue type | Staff can do | Staff must not do | Escalate when |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| iPad / tablet power or battery | Confirm it's on charge; save photo evidence; mark not ready | Open, repair, replace parts, handle unsafe electrical parts | It won't power on / charge after confirming power → ALLCPR + engineer/vendor |

| Facility power / outage | Confirm safety and scope; ask venue to inspect/reset; build packet | Operate breakers; rewire/repair outlets | Outage affects class → venue/property + supervisor |
| Door / access issue | Confirm location; record attempts; mark session not ready | Force doors/locks; guess or share codes | Access blocked / class delayed → venue/property + supervisor |
| Missing supplies | Restock what you have; note and photograph | Make a student responsible for staff restocking | Can't restock on site → ALLCPR |
| Damaged equipment | Photograph; note exact item; mark not ready | Repair or dismantle | Any damage → ALLCPR |
| Refund / cost / insurance / lease / safety / legal | Collect evidence; prepare the escalation packet | Promise or decide any outcome | Always → supervisor / official approval path |
